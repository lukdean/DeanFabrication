3D PRINTER

There are many types and brands of printers, we use a Creality CR6-SE. That means it is a desktop production ready 3D Printer. The extruder moves left and right as well as up and down. The table moves forwards and backwards. Between those three movement directions, all of the necessary motions are able to be performed. The part is formed by extruded softened plastic into beads on the print bed, and slowly building up layer by layer into the final shape.

FILAMENT

FFF or FDM 3D printing requires the use of filament. This is plastic or polymers or polymer composites that have been spun orextruded into longs strands or filaments. These come on spools where they are stored or feed into the machine from. We use four (4) different types of filament: PLA, PETG, PLA+CF, and PETG+CF. Check out the following tables to be able to read more about what each filament is good for and offers your parts.

OPTIONS

Filament Application Colors Pros Cons
PLA (PolyLactic Acid) Great for figurines and low use or low strength parts Black, White, Grey, and Blue Great for beautiful parts. Very easily painted Not the strongest. Does not stand up in the elements
PETG (PolyEthylene Terephthalate Glycol) Great for Low use functional parts. Has a beautiful shine to surface Black, White, Grey, and Blue Much stronger than PLA. Stands up in the elements Very difficult to paint. Can have surface iregularities
PLA+CF (PolyLactic Acid and Carbon Fiber Filler) Great for beautiful structural parts and prototypes Black and Grey Superior strength, elasticity, and dimentionality as well as great finish compared to PLA Slower printing and slightly less detailed
PETG+CF (PolyEthylene Terephthalate Glycol and Carbon Fiber Filler) The strongest of parts that still have a beautiful finish Black and Grey high elasticity, excellent surface finish, dimensional stability, light weight Slower still printing speeds. Lower detail, meant for structural prints
